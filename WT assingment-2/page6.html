Why did NASA start the Apollo program mission?

NASA initiated the Apollo program with the primary goal of landing a man on the Moon and safely returning him to Earth. This ambitious objective was set against the backdrop of the Cold War and the space race between the United States and the Soviet Union. The program was a direct response to the Soviet Union's early successes in space exploration, notably the launch of the first artificial satellite, Sputnik, in 1957, and the first human, Yuri Gagarin, into space in 1961.

These achievements challenged the United States' technological and geopolitical standing. President John F. Kennedy, in a speech to Congress on May 25, 1961, famously declared the goal of sending an American astronaut to the Moon and returning him safely to Earth before the end of the decade. This goal was not only a demonstration of the United States' technological prowess but also a unifying project for the American people and a symbol of American leadership on the global stage.
